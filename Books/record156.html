Synopsis The editor of this anthology, himself a poet and critic, has chosen to select poems that seem to him good rather than to compile a volume that follows the contours of his country's development, the kind of collection that also provides material for the sociologist and historian.  The forty poets included range from Ursula Bethall and Katherine Mansfield, through Allen Curnow and James K. Baxter, to Fleur Adcock and K. O. Arvidson, and each poet is represented by a selection large enough to suggest the variety of his themes ad forms.

No anthology assembled while three-quarters of its contributors are still living, and while many of these are comparatively young, can claim any kind of finality; but Vincent O'Sullivan presents a considered and encouraging report on the state of New Zealand poetry today.

Vincent O'Sullivan is Senior Lecturer in English, University of Waikato
