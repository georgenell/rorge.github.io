Synopsis The passport is a document familiar to all, used and recognized worldwide, and yet it has no basis in law: one country cannot oblige another to admit its subjects simply by issuing a document.  But the state, by insisting on the requirement to hold a passport, provides for itself a neat, self- financing data collection and surveillance system.  This well-illustrated book tells, for the first time, the story of the passport from its earliest origins to the latest high tech Developments.
Handwritten documents adorned with wax seals, modern versions in plastic covers, diplomatic and royal passports and wartime safe concucts, all drawn from the author's collection, complement the exciting exploits of spies and criminals and the tragic real life experiences of regugees.
Whether explaining the introduction of the identity card in revolutionary France, recounting the birth of the British blue passport of the 1920s or divulging the secrets of today's machine readable passport, Marin Lloyd has written an informative and engrossing book which is accessible to everyone.
