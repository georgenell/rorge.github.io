Synopsis <blockquote>'What greater honour could be bestowed on me than to be asked to make myself responsible for the personal safety of this great man'
- Detective Inspector Walter H. Thompson</blockquote>Winson Churchill was a man that many wanted dead.

Scotland Yard detective Walter Thompson was the man charged with ensuring none of them succeeded.

Before the Second World War it was Egyptian Nationalists, Indian Separatists and the IRA who threatened Churchill's life.  During the Second World War it was Hitler, who put a price on Churchill's head.  But all throughout, wherever Churchill went, Thompson went: to guard Britain's war leader - and often to save him from his own recklessness.

During his first retirement, Thompson wrote a book about his inter-war experiences, which Churchill endorsed.  But his second was deemed too revealing and was suppressed, although finally published in censored form.  <i>Churchill's Bodyguard</i> is based on the original manuscript and other unpublished material, some of which Thompson thought too personal to include.

Churchill's Bodyguard offers a unique picture of Winston Churchill and an insight into a relationship that lasted over two decades and became a surprising friendship.
