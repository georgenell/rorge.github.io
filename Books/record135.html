Synopsis This is a nostalgic book for anyone who lived in the times of JFK. It tells the story of a very intelligent man who conquered pain and suffering from war wounds and who triumphed over prejudice to become the first Irish American President of the United States. What is particularly interesting here is the President's rapport with so many different people whether in Boston, New York, Los Angeles, London, Paris, Berlin,or Limerick. He was the same to all and would certainly according to O'Donnell have gone back to Shannon in the springtime. An excellent human story of courage and character.
