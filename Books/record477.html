Synopsis Written in 1887, this classic folk novel of the farmers and fishermen on the Swedish island of Hemsö is one of Strindberg's best.  The story, one of pragmatic marriage, jealousies, and ambition, is enriched by Strindberg's description of nature in the skerries, of sailing, fishing, hunting, farming, and harvesting.  This is a very human novel, and the dialogue has the simplicity and colour typical of people living close to the earth.  Comic and earthy episodes are interwoven with dramatic events, and always the sea is there - to sustain life and to take life.

<i>The Natives of Hemsö</i> has long been one of Strindberg's most widely read works in Europe, but until recent translation into English by Arvid Paulson it was scarcely known here.  The novel shows Strindberg at the top of his forma s a social critic, counterpointing the professional classes and the peasants, exposing the human craving for money and position.
