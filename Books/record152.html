Synopsis Following the success of "Just Walking the Dogs" this second collection of Joe Bennett's columns deals with the horrors of golf and hospital, the pleasures of oxymorons and cricket, the sickness of dogs and apostrophes, and the blights of suburbia from "Star Wars" to karoke, interviews to airports.
Bennett blasts every topic with the limpid comic prose which has led to his columns being syndicated throuhout New Zealand and induced international publishers Simon and Schuster to publish a collection of his columns throughout the rest of the English-speaking world.
Now a freelance writer, Bennett appears with regular irregularity on television and National Radio in the role of the man who knows nothing and says it.
