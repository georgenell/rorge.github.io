Synopsis <i>Shah of Shahs</i> depicts the final years of the Shah in Iran, and is a compelling meditation on the nature of revolution and the devastating results of fear.  Here, Kapuscinski describes the tyrannical monarch, who, despite his cruel oppression of the Iranian people, sees himself as the father of a nation.  Yet even as Iran becomes a 'behemoth of riches' and the Shah lives like a European billionaire, its people live in a climate of fear, terrorized by the secret police.  Told with intense power, Kapuscinski portrays the inevitable build-up to revolution - a cataclysmic upheaval that delivered Iran into the rule of Ayatollah Khomeini.
