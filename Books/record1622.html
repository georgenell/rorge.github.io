Synopsis The Portable Hawthorne includes writings from each major stage in the career of Nathaniel Hawthorne: a number of his most intriguing early tales, all of The Scarlet Letter, excerpts from his three subsequently published romances&mdash;The House of Seven Gables, The Blithedale Romance, and The Marble Faun&mdash;as well as passages from his European journals and a sampling of his last, unfinished works. The editor&rsquo;s introduction and head notes trace the evolution of Hawthorne&rsquo;s writing over the course of his long career: from the tales, to their apotheosis in The Scarlet Letter, through his popular romances, to his private journals and frustrated attempts at another romance. Readers looking for a critical vantage point from which to see Hawthorne whole&mdash;his artistic rise, triumph, and sad decline&mdash;can find it in this collection.
