Synopsis Jonathan Swift (1667-1745) was born in Dublin, where he became Dean of St Patrick's Cathedral.  His savage satires on the British Government's policies towards Ireland earned him the respect of his fellow wits and the love of the Irish people.
<i>Gulliver's Travels</i> is Swift's materpiece - an enthralling and highly entertaining account of gullible Gulliver's travels to such strange lands as Lilliput and Brobdingnag - lands where the people may be large or small, but who are still disturbingly like ourselves in their folly.
Finally, Gulliver visits the land of the Houyhnhnms ('whin-ims') - intelligent horses whose nobility contrasts all too vividly with human stupidity.
