Synopsis As Angus Calder states in his introduction to this edition, '<i>Seven Pillars of Wisdom</i> is one of the major statements about the fighting experience of the First World War'.  Lawrence's younger brothers, Frank and Will, had been killed on the Western Front in 1915.  <i>Seven Pillars of Wisdom</i>, written between 1919 and 1926, tells of the vastly different campaign against the Turks in the Middle East - one which encompasses gross acts of cruelty and revenge and ends in a welter of stink and corpses in the disgusting 'hospital' in Damascus.  <i>Seven Pillars of Wisdom</i> is no <i>Boys Own Paper</i> tale of Imperial triumph, but a complex work of high literary aspiration which stands in the tradition of Melville and Dostoevsky, and alongside the writings of Yeats, Eliot and Joyce.
