Synopsis On 10 July 1985, French secret agents bombed the Greenpeace campaign flagship <i>Rainbow Warrior</i> in Auckland Harbour.  The environmental ship had arrived in New Zealand from Vanuatu to prepare for an anti-nuclear Pacific peace voyage to Moruroa Atoll in French Polynesia.  Portuguese-born photographer Fernando Pereira died in the sabotage outrage that shook the world.  The bombed ship was scuttled off Matauri Bay in 1987 to form a living reef and <i>Rainbow Warrior II</i> was commissioned.  A decade later, French commandos stormed the new <i>Rainbow Warrior</i> in another Moruroa protest and seized the ship.  Under mounting Pacific and global pressure, France abandoned its unpopular nuclear tests the following year.

This is the tale of the last voyage of the bombed ship to the Marshall Islands and the impact of atmospheric nuclear testing on Rongelap Atoll and in French Polynesia.  Now questions hang over the future of both the Rongelapese and Tahitians who are still suffering from nuclear fallout.

The late Farther Walter Hadye Lini, founding prime minister of Vanuatu, wrote the foreword.  Greenpeace campaigner Bunny McDiarmid wrote the 2005 preface.
