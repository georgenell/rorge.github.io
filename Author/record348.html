Synopsis Recipient of the Grand Prix of the Académie Française, <i>Wind, Sand and Stars</i> is unsurpassed in capturing the grandeur, danger, and isolation of flight.  Its exciting account of air adventure - through the treacherous passes of the Pyrenees, above the Sahara, along the snowy ramparts of the Andes - combined with the lyrical prose and soaring spirit of a philosopher, make this book one of the most popular works ever written about flying.

While flying for his air squadron, Antoine De Saint-Exupéry (1900-1944) disappeared over the Mediterranean a year after writing his most famous book, <i>The Little Prince</i>.  He was also the author of <i>Night Flight</i> and <i>Wartime Writings</i>.
