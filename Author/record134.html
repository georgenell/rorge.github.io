Synopsis <i>Above New Zealand</i> is a photographic exploration of New Zealand from the air by Craig Potton, one of the country's most acclaimed landscape photographers.  In this book Craig Potton blends his superb descriptive photography with images in which pattern texture and mood combine to create an outstanding and distinctive interpretation of the New Zealand landscape.

Featuring 130 photographs of both wild and settled parts of the country, these images are the carefully sifted spoils of many years of extensive aerial photography.  The book presents the country in eight regions, and each region is introduced by an essay backgrounding it's natural and cultural landscape.  Photographs are accompanied by informative captions.

First published in 1992, this fourth edition of <i>Above New Zealand</i> has been revised and updated with new photographs.
