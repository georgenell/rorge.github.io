Synopsis During his career as a foreign correspondent, Ryszard Kapuscinski has witnessed twenty-seven revolutions and coups.

<i>Imperium</i> is the story of an empire: the constellation of states that was submerged under a single identity for most of this century - the bureaucratic, amorphous mass of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics.  During the year the Berlin Wall came down, as the empire convulsed and died, Kapuscinski talked to hundreds of ordinary people about their emerging.  <i>Imperium</i> is compelling result of a race against time: to capture people's memories and their fears for the future, before the wonderful, terrible events of 1989 receded into the past.
