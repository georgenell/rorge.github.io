Synopsis Barbara Tuchman is the most successful practising historian in the United States today, and also the best.  <i>Practising History</i> opens with a stimulating discussion of the techniques of writing history and the historian's role.  The pieces that follow show how brilliantly Mrs. Tuchman executes her 'craft', covering subjects as diverse as Henry Kissinger, Mao's China, Israel's Six Day War and Watergate.  Her sure grasp of the vital issues of our age and her views on what history has to teach us combine to make <i>Practising History</i> a splendid and thought-provoking book - a fitting complement to the major works for which Barbara Tuchman is everywhere known and admired.
