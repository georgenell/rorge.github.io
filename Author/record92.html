Synopsis Nine hundred years ago western Europe was ignited by a call to Holy War.  tens of thousands of Christians left their homes, traveling across the face of the known world to recapture the city of Jerusalem from Islam.  For four years these first crusaders endured shattering exhaustion, deadly disease, wretched starvation and bloodthirsty battle on the road to the Holy Land.  Only a fraction survived the journey.

Spurred on by the ravings of fanatical profits, and tattered but hardened core of Crusaders finally rates Jerusalem in 1099.  Once proud knights were reduced to riding donkeys and oxen, but against all odds they succeeded in forging a path through to the world's most sacred city.  The tide of chilling barbarity then unleashed by the pilgrims on their Muslim foes transformed relations between Christiandom and Islam, setting these two great religions on a course towards long-enduring enmity.  Recreating this remarkable endeavour with brilliant scholarship and great narrative flair, Thomas Asbridge has produced a new history of the First Crusade that is unlikely to be surpassed for generations.
