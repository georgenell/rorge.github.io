Synopsis In <i>The Plumb Line</i>, Lord Plumb writes about his father's sudden death in 1952 and reveals: "It was loyalty to the memory of my father that persuaded me to follow in his footsteps" (page 17).

The objective of <i>A Yeoman of England</i> was to surprise Lord Plumb in 2002 with a publication to mark and celebrate the 50th anniversary of his career in agriculture and politics.

The Agricultural Mortgage Corporation PLC and Lloyds TSB Agriculture kindly hosted a reception in London on 21st November 2002 and at this festive occasion, Lord Plumb was presented with the first copy of a collection of essays on a wide variety of topics, dedicated to him by fifty-six distinguished and knowledgeable colleagues and friends from all over the world.

All contributors have played an important role at some stage during his career and <i>A Yeoman of England</i> includes their photographs and short biographies.
