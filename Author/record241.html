Synopsis <i>The Thames</i> provides an intimate portrait of the river's role at the heart of English history, from the dinosaurs on the bank of the prehistoric river to Caesar's expeditionary force; and from King Alfred's battleships to the signing of the Magna Carta; from the coronation and execution of Anne Boleyn to the seventeenth-century frost fairs; and from the first performance of Handel's 'Water Music' right up to the Blitz and the modern transformation of Doclands.

Blending elegant prose with historical detail, this exceptional book brings England's great river superbly to life.
