Synopsis What people say is often very different from what they think or feel; but how do you tell?
The answer lies in the study of body language.  A person's gestures are very accurate indicators of his or her attitudes, thoughts, desires or emotions.

<i>Body Language</i> examines aspects of non-verbal communication in a wide variety of everyday contexts, it discusses techniques that show you how to interpret gestures correctly and this to 'read' the underlying thoughts or emotions.

This book shows:
 - how to tell if your opponent is lying
 - how to get co-operation from other people
 - how negative thoughts are expressed and how to overcome them
 - how to make yourself more credible and likeable
 - how to control an interview, negotiation or conversation
 - how to pick up a partner without being obvious
 - how to avoid a ticket for speeding

<i>Body Language</i> adds a new dimension to the human communication.  It is a must for anybody whose business or personal life involves face-to-face interaction with other people.
