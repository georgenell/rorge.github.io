Synopsis In this Latin translation of <i>The House at Pooh Corner</i>, Brian Staples has found ways of rendering A.A. Milne's particular style; the puns, special use of words and all the Hums, which are such a feature of the original stories.  The result is a happy variation of the well-loved stories which will appeal not only to Latin students, but also to those fond of language and Winnie-the-Pooh.

'Paene undecima hora,' dixit Pu felix, 'in tempore opportuno buccellae es' - and here is a delightful little something... we find Pu and Porcellus and Tigris and Ior and Lepus all leading their amiable lives in the language of Tacitus and Virgil... Together with all the familiar drawings by E.H. Shepard, this makes unexpectedly easy reading - and happily even the dimmest classicist may believe he remembers more Latin than he ever knew.
